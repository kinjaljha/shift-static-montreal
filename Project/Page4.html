Structural barriers to the city's
ability to support communities

“They can’t help us because of some rule, and they can't change that rule because they're caught in their rigid structure. It's always more paper, more sticks in the wheels … This would be a good time to think about how to change that to serve the citizens more.” (#17)

What Can We Do?

Responding to 5 Challenges

Overall, there is a shared sense that to build a more resilient, just and sustainable city, the city and borough governments and communities need to be able to communicate and coordinate more effectively in times of crisis, in order to transform structures that are not working. Many of the community organizers welcome the opportunity to meet with city officials to think together about how to improve relations, about how to work towards a much more effective and fair sharing of information and resources. This is the work we will do together on June 30.
